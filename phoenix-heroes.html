🎭 Rarity & the troop triangle

Rare (blue) heroes have 4 skills, Epic (purple) have 5, Mythic (gold) have 6 plus 2 more on their exclusive weapon. Every hero is Infantry, Lancer, or Marksman, and there's a damage triangle between them: Infantry hit Lancers harder, Lancers hit Marksmen harder, Marksmen hit Infantry harder. Mixing all three troop types on a march means you're always covered.

🗺️ Two separate skill sets

Every hero has an Exploration set (left side of their page) and an Expedition set (right side) — they don't work in the same places:

  • Exploration skills apply to Exploration, Arena, and Lighthouse/Intel 5v5 battles.
  • Expedition skills apply to real deployments — Polar Terror rallies, beast hunts, city attacks, fortress and castle fights.

Mythic exclusive weapons follow the same split: odd upgrade levels (1,3,5,7,9) boost the Exploration side, even levels (2,4,6,8,10) boost the Expedition side. A level 10 weapon costs 275 widgets total.

⭐ Leveling vs. ascending

These are two different systems, and mixing them up wastes resources:

  • EXP (level 1–80) raises escort stats and deployment capacity. Your Furnace level caps how high you can go. Focus your best 5 heroes.
  • Shards (star level) unlock new skill slots and let you upgrade skills further. Each hero needs 10 shards just to unlock, then far more to push stars — roughly 1,075 shards total to fully ascend one hero to 4 stars. Don't blow General Shards on heroes you're not committed to.

At Furnace 13 the Drill Camp unlocks: park low-level heroes there and they auto-level to match your 5th-strongest hero, which is a good way to bring backup heroes up to speed without babysitting them. If you ever want to swap your top 5, resetting a hero in the Drill Camp refunds 100% of its EXP.

🔨 "Growth" skills — always-on economy buffs

Some skills quietly boost your city all the time, not just in battle — look for the green hammer icon next to a hero's troop type. All four Rare heroes have one (a resource-gathering buff plus a matching income buff). A few notable ones from later heroes: construction cost/speed, research speed, infirmary healing speed, and training speed. A couple of heroes also buff march speed or stamina cost for a rally they're part of. Worth leveling early since the effect runs constantly.

🚩 Rally leader vs. joiner buffs

This one trips a lot of people up: when you lead a rally, all of your heroes' buffs apply. When you join someone else's rally, only your top-right Expedition skill counts — the rest of your hero lineup doesn't matter for that march. That's why attack-focused heroes (e.g. Jessie) are the go-to for attacking, and defense/garrison-focused heroes (e.g. Patrick, Sergey) are the go-to for holding a fortress or castle. Inside a garrison, the strongest hero lineup present takes priority for buffs — not necessarily whoever is nominally "leading" it.
